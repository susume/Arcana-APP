Tarot Reading Guide

What is tarot?

Tarot is a 78-card symbolic system used for reflection, insight, and guidance. A tarot reading does not need to predict a fixed future; it helps you examine a question, notice patterns, and consider possible next steps.

How does Arcana create an AI tarot reading?

Arcana reads the spread you create with your own cards. You choose a spread, draw and place real cards, upload a photo or enter the cards manually, then receive an interpretation based on each card and its position.

What are the Major and Minor Arcana?

The 22 Major Arcana cards represent major life themes, turning points, and lessons. The 56 Minor Arcana cards describe everyday situations through four suits: Wands, Cups, Swords, and Pentacles.

What do the tarot suits mean?

Wands often point to action, creativity, and ambition. Cups relate to emotion, intuition, and relationships. Swords explore thought, truth, conflict, and clarity. Pentacles connect to work, money, body, home, and material life.

What do court cards mean in tarot?

Court cards often describe a person, role, attitude, or stage of growth. Pages suggest learning and messages, Knights suggest movement, Queens suggest inner mastery, and Kings suggest leadership or responsibility.

What does a reversed tarot card mean?

A reversed card appears upside-down and usually points to blocked, delayed, internalized, or shadow-side energy. It is not automatically negative; it adds nuance to the card's upright meaning.

What is a dropped or jumper card?

A dropped card is a card that falls out while shuffling. Many readers treat it as an extra message or underlying theme, so Arcana lets you record it separately from the main spread.

Which tarot spread should beginners choose?

Beginners should start with one card for a daily focus or three cards for past, present, and future. Larger spreads are better when you already have a clear question and want more detail.

What is the difference between AI and classic readings?

AI readings use Google Gemini to create a personalized narrative from your cards, spread positions, and optional context. Classic readings use built-in card meanings and work offline without an API key.
